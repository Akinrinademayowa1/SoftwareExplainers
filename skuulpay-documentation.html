SkuulPay is the fee payment and financial record system your school runs on — from assigning a class's fees for the term, to collecting payment, to reconciling the very last outstanding balance. This guide covers every screen, for the administrators running it and the parents paying through it.

Getting started

SkuulPay runs as two connected portals sharing one live record of every fee, payment, and balance: an Admin Portal for the school, and a Parent & Student Portal for whoever is paying.

The two portals

Admin Portal

Used by the school. Set up sessions and terms, assign fees per class, record payments, apply discounts, and see the school's full financial picture.

Parent & Student Portal

Used by whoever pays. See what's owed, pay it, and keep a receipt-backed record of every payment ever made.

Setting up your school

  1. Create your school's account. Provide your school's name, a username, an email address, and a password. Your username doubles as your School Code — the code students and parents will enter to find your school when they sign in.
  2. Sign in to the Admin Portal with the email and password you just chose.
  3. Add a session and at least one term under Sessions & Terms — everything else in SkuulPay is organized around these.
  4. Turn on the payment categories your school charges for under Payment Categories, then set an amount for each class under Class Fees.
  5. Add your students and enroll them into a class for the current term — from that point on, they'll owe exactly what you set, automatically.
Tip

Not ready to add real students yet? Open Students Fill Test Data to instantly populate a handful of example students with fees and enrollments across every session and term — enough to click through the entire product first. Clear Test Data removes exactly what was added, and only that, whenever you're ready to go live.

Overview

Dashboard

The first screen after signing in — a real-time snapshot of the school's finances for whichever session and term you're looking at.

What's on it

  • Total Collected — everything received so far, cash and card combined.
  • Outstanding Balance — everything still owed across every enrolled student.
  • Active Students and Payments Made — headline counts for the period.
  • A running feed of recent activity — fees assigned, payments approved, discounts applied.

Filtering by session and term

A filter at the top of the screen narrows every number on the Dashboard — and on Analytics, Payments, the Ledger, and Enrollments — to a single session and term. Leave it on All to see the school's numbers overall, or pin it to one term to see exactly that period in isolation.

Management

Enrollments

Enrollment is what connects a student to a class for a specific session and term — and it's what makes that class's fees actually apply to them.

A student can be added to the school without being enrolled anywhere yet; they won't owe anything until they're enrolled in a class for a term. Use Bulk Enroll to enroll a whole class at once instead of one student at a time — useful at the start of every new term.

Note

Re-enrolling students each new term is what carries them into that term's fee cycle — a student enrolled last term isn't automatically enrolled this term.

Management

Sessions & Terms

Every fee, payment, and balance in SkuulPay belongs to a specific session and term — this is the calendar the rest of the product is built around.

  • A session is an academic year, e.g. 2024/2025.
  • A term is a period within it, e.g. First Term, Second Term.
  • Exactly one term is marked active at a time — it's what every screen defaults to, and what a parent's payment applies to unless a past balance takes priority (more on that under Financial Ledger).

Keeping every record scoped to a session and term is what lets you look back at any past period cleanly — nothing from two terms ago gets mixed into this term's numbers.

Management

Payment Categories

Categories are the types of fee your school charges — tuition, feeding, transport, books, or anything else you bill for.

Create as many categories as your school needs and enable the ones currently in use. Only enabled categories can have an amount set against them under Class Fees, so disabling one you no longer charge for keeps it out of new fee assignments without deleting its history.

Management

Class Fees

This is where an amount actually gets attached to a class — set it once, and every student enrolled in that class for that term owes it automatically.

A fee is defined by four things: a class, a session, a term, and a category. That combination can carry its own amount — so JSS 1's tuition for First Term can be set independently of JSS 1's tuition for Second Term, or of SS 3's tuition altogether.

Finance

Payments

Money reaches SkuulPay one of two ways: a parent pays through the Parent Portal, or the school records a payment it received directly.

Online payments

Parents pay through the Parent Portal using a card via your configured payment gateway. Once a payment clears, it's approved automatically, a receipt is generated, and the student's balance updates immediately.

Recording a cash or bank payment

For anything paid directly to the school — cash at the office, a bank transfer, a cheque — use Record Cash Payment from a student's profile or the Payments screen. Enter the amount, category, and a note; it's logged exactly like an online payment, receipt included.

Payment statuses

StatusMeaning

Filter the Payments screen by class, category, or status to find any transaction quickly.

Finance

Financial Ledger

The Ledger is the single running record behind every number in SkuulPay — every fee assigned, every payment received, and every discount applied, for every student, permanently.

How a payment settles a balance

When a payment comes in, it's applied to the oldest outstanding balance first — automatically. If a student owed money from last term and pays this term, that payment clears last term's balance before it touches this term's. Nothing needs to be told which term a payment is "for"; the Ledger works it out.

Finance

Discounts

Define a discount once — a name and an amount — then apply it to the students who qualify, one at a time or all at once.

Creating a discount

A discount is just a name ("Staff Children," "Merit Scholarship," "Sibling Discount") and a fixed amount. Once created, it can be assigned to any student, against any category, for any term.

Assigning to one student

Use Assign next to a discount to apply it to a single student for a specific session, term, and category.

Bulk Assign

Use Bulk Assign to apply the same discount to many students across many terms in one action — for example, giving every staff member's child a discount across the entire academic year in a single step, instead of assigning it one student and one term at a time.

Good to know

A discount can never bring a student's balance below zero — if it would exceed what's actually owed for that term and category, that one is skipped and listed for you, while everyone else in the batch still goes through.

Communication

Notifications

Both portals keep a notification feed so nothing gets missed.

Students and parents are notified the moment a fee is assigned to them and the moment a payment is approved. Administrators see notifications for anything that needs attention — a payment awaiting confirmation, for instance. Every notification is available from the bell icon in either portal.

Help

Frequently asked questions

What happens if a payment is more than what's owed this term?

It's applied to the oldest outstanding balance first. If nothing older is owed, the extra is recorded against the current term as an advance payment.

Can more than one person manage the school's account?

Each school has one administrator sign-in, used by whoever manages fees and records payments — your bursar, proprietor, or office manager.

Can I change our school's currency later?

Yes — update it any time in Settings. The symbol updates across both portals, including past receipts.

A parent doesn't have the Student ID — what do they do?

Any administrator can look it up from that student's profile under Students and share it with the parent directly.

Is our school's data kept separate from other schools?

Yes. Every school's records are kept entirely separate from every other school using SkuulPay, behind its own sign-in.

What's the difference between a Session and a Term?

A session is the full academic year; a term is one period inside it. Fees, payments, and balances are always tied to both.
