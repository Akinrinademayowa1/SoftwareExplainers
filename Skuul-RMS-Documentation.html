What Skuul-RMS does

Skuul-RMS is a results management platform built for schools that want one dependable place to handle assessment data — from the first continuous assessment score entered in a term to the certificate a top student downloads at the end of a session.

It's built around a simple idea: a school's academic record should be structured, searchable, and easy to hand to the people who need it — administrators generating report cards, teachers reviewing class performance, and students checking their own results — without anyone needing to touch a spreadsheet formula.

Who uses it

School Administrators

Configure the school profile, manage sessions and classes, upload assessment scores, and generate report cards and certificates.

Students

Look up their own results by school code and student ID, view detailed performance analytics, and download their own report cards.

Every School, Every System

Grading weights, class names, sessions, and certificate branding are all configurable — Skuul-RMS adapts to how your school already grades, not the other way around.

Core concepts

A handful of terms recur throughout this documentation. Getting comfortable with them up front makes everything else click faster.

TermWhat it means
SessionAn academic year, e.g. 2025/2026. Your school can have many sessions on record, but only one is active for new uploads at a time.
TermA grading period inside a session — First Term, Second Term, and so on. Each session can hold as many terms as your school uses.
ClassA group of students, e.g. Grade 9A. Classes are configured once and then selected from a dropdown every time you upload results.
School CodeA short, unique code (like GHS-2026) that identifies your school. Students use it to find your school's portal before searching for their own results.

Getting started

Setting up your school takes about ten minutes. Here's the full path from a blank account to your first uploaded result.

Creating your school account

From the sign-in screen, switch to the Create Account tab and fill in:

  1. School Name — your school's full name, exactly as you want it to appear on report cards and certificates.
  2. School Code — auto-suggested from your school name, but you can set your own. This is what students will enter to reach your portal, so keep it short and easy to share (e.g. GHS-2026).
  3. Admin Username — the name you'll sign in with.
  4. Email Address — required for every account. Used to verify your identity at sign-in.
  5. Password — at least 6 characters. You'll confirm it once more before the account is created.
Username, email, and password — all three, every time

Signing in asks for your School Code, Username, Email, and Password together. This four-part check keeps each school's dashboard tied specifically to the people who should have access to it.

Signing in

Return to the Login tab and enter your School Code, Username, Email, and Password. Once verified, you're taken straight to your Dashboard.

Your first-time setup checklist

Before uploading any results, walk through Configuration once. Everything here can be changed later, but getting it right at the start saves re-work.

1. School profile

Name, type, address, phone, and logo.

2. Sessions & terms

Create your academic session and its terms, then activate one.

3. Classes

Add every class your school teaches, so they're ready in the upload dropdown.

4. Grading structure

Set how much each assessment (1st test, 2nd test, exam) is worth.

5. Certificate design

Choose the look your achievement certificates will use.

6. Upload results

You're ready — head to Upload Results and bring in your first CSV files.

Admin Dashboard

Dashboard

Your Dashboard is the first thing you see after signing in, and it's built to answer "how is this term going?" at a glance before you dig into any one class or student.

Overview cards

Four cards summarize the active session and term: Total Students, Subjects, Reports Generated, and Upload Progress — the last of which fills in as you complete each stage of the upload process (first test, second test, exam).

Below them, a second row shows Students Passed, Students Failed, and the Class Average for whichever session and term is currently active.

Interactive charts

  • Grade Distribution — a doughnut chart breaking down how many students landed in each grade band across all uploaded subjects.
  • Top 4 Student Performance — a bar chart of your four highest-scoring students for the active term, colour-coded by how strong the result is.

Both charts update immediately as new results are uploaded and compiled — there's no separate "refresh" step.

Achievements & Top Performers

A tabbed panel surfaces your school's best performances across five different lenses, each explained in full under Achievements & Certificates:

🏅Best of Session 📅Best of Term 🏫Best of Class ⭐All-Time Best

A fifth tab, Subject Champions, shows the highest scorer in every subject your school has ever recorded results for.

Quick Actions & Recent Activity

Quick Actions link straight to Configuration, Upload Results, and report generation. Recent Activity keeps a running log of what's happened most recently — new uploads, configuration changes, generated reports — so you always know what the last person to touch the dashboard did.

Admin Dashboard

Configuration

Configuration is where your school's identity, grading rules, and document branding all live. Every setting here feeds directly into the report cards and certificates your school produces.

School profile

Set your school name, type or level (this appears on the report card title row), address, and telephone number. Your school subheading — the line shown under your school name on report cards — is built from a set of selectable tags (Nursery, Primary, Secondary, College, and so on); pick as many as apply, or add your own custom label if none quite fit.

Next term dates

Set the dates your next term begins and ends. These print automatically on every report card, so parents and students always know when to expect the school to reopen.

Sessions & terms

Add a new session (e.g. 2026/2027), then add terms inside it. Only one session and one term can be active at any time — that's the combination new uploads are saved under, and the combination new students see by default in results.

Activating a new term doesn't erase the old one

Every session and term you've ever created stays on record. Switching the active term only changes where new uploads go — past results remain fully searchable and downloadable.

Classes

Add every class your school runs — Grade 9A, Grade 10B, whatever naming your school already uses. Once added, classes appear as a dropdown when uploading results, so there's no risk of a typo creating two different classes that were meant to be the same one.

Certificate design

Choose the visual style used for every achievement certificate your school generates. Your selection applies immediately to all future certificate downloads.

Classic Gold

Ornate gold double border with a ribbon medallion seal — timeless and formal.

Aurora Gradient

A vibrant teal-to-violet-to-pink gradient band — modern and colourful.

Emerald Frame

A bold emerald-and-gold frame with a matching signature band along the bottom.

Branding: logo & signatures

Upload your school logo, principal's signature, and head teacher's signature once, and they'll appear automatically on every report card and certificate from then on. Images are optimised on upload, so there's no need to resize anything yourself beforehand.

Grading structure

By default, scores are split 20 / 20 / 60 across the first test, second test, and exam. If your school weighs these differently — say, giving more weight to the final exam — you can set your own split, as long as the three numbers add up to exactly 100.

Example — exam-weighted structure
1st Test:  10 marks
2nd Test:  20 marks
Exam:      70 marks
─────────────────
Total:    100 marks
Confirmed before it applies

Changing your grading structure shows a before-and-after summary that you must confirm. Already-uploaded scores keep the values they were recorded with — the new structure applies to new uploads going forward.

Saving changes

Clicking Save Configuration opens a summary of every change you're about to make — school details, subheading, dates, certificate design, and any new logo or signature files — so nothing is applied until you've reviewed and confirmed it.

Admin Dashboard

Uploading results

Results are brought in as CSV files — one file per subject, uploaded together in three passes across a term.

Preparing your files

Each CSV needs four columns: StudentID, StudentName, Subject, and Score. If your file is named after the subject (e.g. Mathematics.csv), Skuul-RMS will pick that up automatically even if the Subject column is left blank.

Mathematics.csv
StudentID,StudentName,Subject,Score
STD-1001,Amelia Chen,Mathematics,18
STD-1002,Lucas Martin,Mathematics,16
STD-1003,Fatima Hassan,Mathematics,19
STD-1004,Noah Kim,Mathematics,14

Not sure of the exact format? Download the sample CSV templates directly from the Upload Results page — they're pre-filled with example data matching this structure exactly.

The four-step process

  1. First Test — select the class from the dropdown, then upload every subject's CSV file for the first continuous assessment.
  2. Second Test — upload the same subjects' files for the second continuous assessment.
  3. Exam — upload the final exam scores for each subject.
  4. Generate — review the compiled results across all three uploads, then generate report cards for the whole class at once.

Score validation

Every score is automatically checked against your school's configured grading structure. A first-test score that exceeds its configured maximum is clamped down to that maximum, and you're shown a summary of exactly which rows were adjusted before the upload completes.

Handling class conflicts

If a student ID already has results recorded under a different class for the session and term you're uploading into, Skuul-RMS pauses and shows you exactly which students are affected, with their existing class and the new upload's class side by side. You choose how to resolve it:

Skip Conflicts

Keep existing records untouched; only add genuinely new students from this upload.

Replace All

Overwrite the conflicting records so they now belong to the new upload's class.

Cancel Upload

Stop and make no changes, so you can double-check the file before trying again.

How positions are calculated

Class position is ranked by total aggregate score, using dense ranking: students who tie share the same position, and the next distinct score takes the very next position — no position is ever skipped.

Scores:     95   95   90   85   85   80
Positions:  1st  1st  2nd  3rd  3rd  4th

Two students tied for 1st simply means there's no 2nd place taken — the next student is correctly 2nd, not 3rd.

Downloading

From the report card preview, download as a PDF for printing or sharing, or as a Word document if the school needs to make further edits — both reflect exactly what's shown in the preview, at full quality, regardless of the device you're downloading from.

Admin Dashboard

Managing reports

Every student record ever generated lives in Manage Reports, filterable by session, term, and class.

  • Filter — narrow the list down to a specific session, term, or class before searching further by name.
  • View & download — open any student's report card directly from the table, in PDF or Word.
  • Delete — remove an individual student's record for a given session and term, with a confirmation step first.

The table itself scrolls independently within a fixed-height panel, so a long list of records never pushes the rest of the page out of view.

Admin Dashboard

Achievements & certificates

Beyond individual report cards, Skuul-RMS surfaces the standout performances across your whole school and lets you turn any of them into a downloadable certificate in a couple of clicks.

The five achievement categories

Best of Session

Top 4 students across all classes and terms within one academic session, ranked by average performance.

Best of Term

Top 4 students across all classes for one specific term.

Best of Class

Top 4 students of one specific class, within one session.

All-Time Best

Top 4 students across every session and term your school has ever recorded.

Subject Champions

The highest all-time scorer in every individual subject your school teaches.

Every category uses the same dense ranking as report card positions — if two students are tied for a spot, both are recognised, and the list simply includes everyone who's genuinely in the top four positions.

Downloading certificates

Each name in an achievement list has two buttons: Report, which opens that student's report card for the relevant term, and Certificate, which generates a certificate using your school's chosen design, complete with your logo, signatures, and the specific achievement being recognised.

Certificates always match what you preview

Whether you're downloading from a laptop or a phone, the certificate PDF is generated at full design resolution every time — what you see in the preview is exactly what gets saved.

Students

Student portal

Students don't need an account to check their results — just their school's code and their own student ID.

Finding your school

From the portal's entry screen, a student enters their School Code (available from their teacher or school notice board) to reach their school's dedicated results search page.

Searching for results

Students can narrow their search by session and term, then look themselves up by student ID or name. Leaving the session and term filters on their default setting searches across every record on file for that student.

Understanding the results page

Once a result is found, students see a complete performance breakdown:

  • Key stats — aggregate score, average percentage, class position, and pass rate.
  • Highlights — strongest subjects, subjects needing improvement, a pass/fail summary, and a consistency reading.
  • Four charts — a subject score comparison, grade distribution, an assessment breakdown (1st test / 2nd test / exam), and a performance radar across subjects.
  • Term-by-term progress — a simple bar comparison of average performance across every term on record, so improvement (or decline) is visible at a glance.
  • Improvement recommendations — plain-language suggestions generated from the student's own results.

Your achievements

If a student qualifies for any of the five achievement categories, a dedicated section appears right on their results page, with the same Report and Certificate download buttons available to administrators — students can download their own certificates the moment they discover they've earned one.

Account

Security

Every school's data is fully isolated from every other school's — nothing you upload, configure, or generate is ever visible outside your own account.

Updating your credentials

From the Security page, update your username, email, or password. Any change requires your current password first, and a new password must be at least 6 characters.

Danger Zone

If you ever need to start completely fresh, Danger Zone permanently wipes every uploaded result, generated report, and configuration setting — while keeping your login account intact so you don't need to register again.

This cannot be undone

To prevent accidental data loss, you must type the exact confirmation phrase shown on screen before the reset button becomes active. There's no way to recover data once it's cleared.

Account

Activity log

Every meaningful action — uploads, configuration changes, generated reports, credential updates — is timestamped and recorded in your Activity Log, giving you a running history of everything that's happened on your account. The log itself scrolls within a fixed-height panel and can be cleared at any time.

Account

Appearance

A dark mode toggle sits in the sidebar of your dashboard. Switching it applies instantly across every page — dashboard, configuration, upload, reports — and your preference is remembered the next time you sign in. Report cards and certificates always render on a light background regardless of your theme choice, since they're built to be printed and read on paper.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use my own grading scale?
Yes. The weight given to each assessment (1st test, 2nd test, exam) is fully configurable, as long as the three values add up to 100. The letter-grade bands (A/B/C/D/F) are the standard scale used to interpret the resulting total.
What happens when two students score exactly the same?
They share the same position, and the next distinct score takes the very next position — no gaps are left behind a tie. This applies to class positions, achievement rankings, and certificates alike.
Can students see each other's results?
No. A student can only retrieve results by entering the exact student ID (or matching name) they're searching for — there's no listing or browsing of other students' records.
Can I change the grading structure partway through a term?
Yes, though it's worth timing carefully — already-uploaded scores keep the values they were recorded with, while any assessment you upload after the change follows the new structure.
What formats can I download report cards and certificates in?
Report cards download as PDF or Word. Certificates download as PDF. Both are generated at full design resolution regardless of the device you're using.
Is my school's data kept separate from other schools using Skuul-RMS?
Yes, completely. Every school's configuration, results, and reports are isolated to that school's own account and are never visible to any other school on the platform.
